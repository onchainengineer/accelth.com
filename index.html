Issues plaguing our healthcare system

India’s healthcare system has been battling various issues for quite a while now. There’s a need to make people and processes in the healthcare sector more accountable; for that greater operational finesse needs to be brought in urgently.

Lack of infrastructure

Over 75% of the healthcare infrastructure is concentrated in metro cities, where only 27% of the total population resides—the rest 73% of the Indian population lack even basic medical facilities.

Shortage of trained manpower

The primary medical centers are lacking over 3,000 doctors and in the last decade, the shortage has increased by ~200%.

Inconsistent health records

The records form a permanent account of a patient's illness. Their clarity and accuracy are paramount for effective communication between healthcare professionals and patients.

Unaffordable Prices

Since the public health sector is less funded, the arena of healthcare is extremely capitalized. Due to the nature of a free economy in the healthcare sector, with extremely high demand, the prices of private healthcare in India continue to boom at an unprecedented rate.

Lack of proactive healthcare

Proactive healthcare gives patients and clinicians earlier and easier access to critical health information that can be used to manage conditions before symptoms appear, crises occur, and the prognosis is poor.

Unavailablity of on-time delivery

Today in India medicines are mostly regionalised - out of more than 650+ branded manufacturers, only approximate 200+ manufacturers medicine are available in a state / city.
